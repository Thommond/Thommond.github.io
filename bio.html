I was just conversing with a peer of mine, he stated "AI will save us and destroy us. They will cure cancer and old age." I attempted to explain to him that in no circumstance that computers or AI could cure cancer or end humanity. He did not want to hear that computers are dumb. He and others have a superhero or supervillain like view when AI is brought in on a conversation. He then explained to me the his worries about self-driving cars. He exclaimed, "I would never let a machine drive a car for me!". I said, "Why, not they can provide a safer alternative to human drivers". In the same conversation, two stigmas about computers and machines were brought to the surface. Moreover, The first stigma is the thought that computers can and "will" solve every problem. Computers are great but, like humans, they have their flaws. For example, a computer know's only what we tell the computer. If the boot is equal to one then that is true. Boot is nothing else to the computer unless we say it is. The mysterious legacy of the computer may never die, but it is our job as programmers to keep the beast at bay. Second is the fact that computers are untrustworthy. It seems almost contradictory, computers are going to take over humanity but, I will never trust one to drive! And the latter computers are dumb but they can be trusted Remember, I said computers only do what we tell them to. So, we can expect and predict what they will do. For example, Tesla uses video data from thousands of its customers to achieve a fully autonomous vehicle. Using billions of bytes of data Tesla Co tell's the computer what turns to make and when, when to stop, go, increase speed and so on. Computers have a bright future but, the public tends to get carried away. It is more important than ever to change the public view on AI; because it is only becoming more prevalent
